But in sentences without a finite verb the personal pronoun denoting the subject cannot be missing. It may be said promiscuously kṛtavān asmi} and kṛtavān aham} and kṛtakṛtyo ’si} and so on. The full forms ahaṃ kṛtavān asmi}, tvaṃ kṛtakṛtyo ’si} are, of course, also available.

Rem. Occasionally they are wanting even then, provided that it be beyond doubt, which subject is meant. Panc. 214 the crow Sthirajîvin relates to the king of the owls the ill treatment he has endured from his own king, for tena durjanaprakopitenemāṃ daśāṃ nītaḥ} [sc. aham}, as is perspicuous by the context]; ibid. 53 the lover addresses the princess rājaputri suptā [sc. tvaṃ}] kiṃ vā jāgarṣi}; ibid. 38 vatsa dhanyo ’si yatprathame vayasyevaṃ viraktibhāvaḥ} [sc. tvam}]. Cp. ibid. 137, 13; 154, 10.
