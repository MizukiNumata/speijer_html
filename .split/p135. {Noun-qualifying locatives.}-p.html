135. {Noun-qualifying locatives.}

According to what has been said 111, it is plain, that nothing impedes locatives qualifying a noun. Such phrases as [kUpe salilam / nadyAM naukA] are as good Sanskrit as „water in the pit,” „a boat on the river” are good English. — Here the genitive is concurrent.

In some turns the locative is standing, as in divisions of literary works as [iti zrImadrAmAyaNe vAlmikIya araNyakhaNDe prathamaH sargaH], we say, the first sarga of the Âranyakhanḍa of the Râmayana of Valmîki.
