163. {[adhaH] and [adhastAt]}.

[2.] [adhaH] (below, under). Its synonym is [adhastAt], a derivative of it. Both are construed with preceding genitive. Çâk. I [nIvArAH…bhraSTAstarUNAmadhaH], Panc. 145 [asyaiva nyagrodhasyAdhastAtprakSipya mAm].

Rem. 1. Sometimes it complies with abl. Panc. 145 [vRkSAdadhaH]. Compare 171 R.

Rem. 2 . To denote a lower place or state the old dialect possessed also the adverbs [avaH] and [avastAt](*1). Çat. Br. 9, 3, 1, 6 [avastAddhidiva AdityaH]

(*1)

Comp. the upasarga [ava] and lacus Avernus, the Latin designation of the regions below.
