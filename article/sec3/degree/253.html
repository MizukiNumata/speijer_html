253. The type manus manum lavat and the like.

Sanskrit likes juxtaposition of different grammatical forms of the same word or of kindred words. Hence the type manus manum lavat is of course very common in Sanskrit. Mrcch. I, p. 84 ratnaṃ ratnena saṃgacchate} (pearls string with pearls), Vikram. II, p. 31 taptena taptamayasā ghaṭanāya yogyam}; Pat. I, p. 233 vāso vāsaś chādayati} (one cloth covers the other), Panc. 322 vanādvanaṃ paryaṭati} (he rambles from forest to forest), ibid. 267 padāt padam api pracalituṃ na śaknoti}, Daç, 61 kariṇaḥ kariṇam avaplutya} (jumping from one elephant’s back on another).
